The Founding

It Started with a Broken Bat

Harold King was a middle-order batsman of modest ability but obsessive attention to equipment. In 1977, after snapping his third bought bat in a single Essex League season, he decided to make his own.

What began in a garage with a draw knife, a hand plane, and willow sourced from a farmer in Chelmsford became KingsWillow Craft Co. — today a three-generation workshop producing over 250 bats a year, still from the same address.

Harold's son Robert took over the workshop in 2001. His daughter Ellie joined as head of finishing and design in 2018. Between them they carry 70 years of combined bat-making experience.

What We Stand For

The KingsWillow Principles

🌲

Willow Conservation

Partnered with Essex Willow Growers since 1984. We replant 3 cricket willows for every cleft we harvest, and support 4 grower families year-round.

🏏

Sport First

We don't make bats to maximise margin. We make them to perform. Every specification decision starts with what helps the batter, not what's cheapest to produce.

🔧

Craft, Not Commerce

We cap production at 300 bats per season. We will not scale beyond what our craftsmen can make well. When demand exceeds capacity, we wait — we don't compromise.

🧒

Youth Commitment

10% of all Colt junior bats are donated to underfunded county cricket academies each year. Since 2010, we've donated over 400 bats.

Our History

From Garage to Workshop

1977

Harold builds his first bat in a Chelmsford garage

First five bats sold to teammates at Chelmsford CC. The workshop is born from necessity and stubbornness.

1984

First willow growing partnership established

Harold forms a direct agreement with the Blackwood family farm in Essex — a relationship that continues to this day.

1991

First county cricketer uses a KingsWillow bat

Essex Second XI opening batsman Alan Pritt scores 187* with a Monarch prototype. Word spreads through the county circuit.

2001

Robert King joins full-time; workshop moves to current site

New 3,200 sq ft workshop in Chelmsford allows for proper seasoning rooms and the first mechanical pressing rig.

2010

Junior donation programme launches

The Colt range is created and a commitment made to donate 10% of production to youth cricket academies.

2018

Ellie King joins; Sovereign bespoke range launched

Limited to 50 bats per season. The first run sells out in 11 days. Waiting list for the 2019 season opens immediately.

2024

12,000th bat rolls off the bench

A Grade 1+ Sovereign, commissioned by a Hampshire batsman whose father bought bat #1,000 in 1999.
